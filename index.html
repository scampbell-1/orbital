About the Novel

Orbital is Samantha Harvey's fifth novel. Set entirely on the International Space Station, it takes place over a single day — twenty-four hours, sixteen orbits of Earth. Six crew members from different nations float in that extraordinary vantage point: watching storms build over the Pacific, wildfires crawl across continents, and the slow arc of day and night cycling every forty-five minutes.

The novel has almost no plot in any conventional sense. There is no villain, no crisis, no resolution. Instead, Harvey gives us something rarer: sustained attention to what it feels like to be alive — and to witness human life from a distance so vast that borders disappear, wars shrink to silence, and the fragile blue line of atmosphere becomes the most terrifying and beautiful thing any of them has ever seen.

It won the Booker Prize in 2024, with judges praising its "quality of attention" and the audacity of its form. It is spare, lyrical, and profoundly moving.

✦

About the Author

Samantha Harvey is a British novelist and Professor of Creative Writing at Bath Spa University. Her previous novels include The Wilderness (2009), All Is Song (2012), Dear Thief (2014), and The Western Wind (2018). She is also known for her memoir on insomnia, The Shapeless Unease (2020).

Harvey has spoken in interviews about writing Orbital during a period of intense personal and global uncertainty — the pandemic years — when the question of what humans are doing to the planet felt especially urgent. The astronauts' view from above became a way to hold that question without easy answers.

The Story at a Glance

Six astronauts and cosmonauts — representing Russia, America, Britain, Italy, and Japan — orbit Earth for one complete day. Each brings a private life with them: an ill wife, a dying mother, a longing for home, a career drawing to its end. Their routines are precise: maintenance tasks, scientific experiments, meals eaten from pouches, sleep in narrow bunks tethered against floating away.

Meanwhile, below: a typhoon builds over the Pacific. Wars continue. A body is buried. People are born. The crew watches all of this — and cannot intervene in any of it.

The novel moves between the intimate interior lives of its characters and the overwhelming scale of what they observe. Harvey is interested in what happens when human beings are removed from the context of their ordinary lives and forced to see the whole of existence — including their own smallness — from outside it.

VCE English Context

Unit 3 — Reading and Responding to Texts

Orbital appears on the VCE English text list as a set text for Unit 3, Area of Study 1. Students are required to read and respond analytically to the text, demonstrating an understanding of how Harvey constructs meaning through her choices of form, structure, and language.

Your analytical writing should address how Harvey uses specific literary techniques to develop ideas, construct characters, and position readers — not merely what the text is about, but how it achieves its effects.
